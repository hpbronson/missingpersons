Real women. Real stories.

Sisters Brittney Garcia and Brandi Funk are survivors of human trafficking. They are proof that stories can be changed.

Story taken from Deseret News.

The Aspen House is a newly founded organization who's purpose is intended to help survivors of human trafficking get back on their feet. One of the members of the board, Brittney Garcia, adds that this house is intended to help women with trauma counseling, addiction help, and job training.

Brittney and her sister, Brandi, both became victimized at the same time, each in their early 30s. Additionally, both met a man who were "grooming" them to be trafficked. Unfortunately both were unable to get out of their situations until getting arrested and sought help.






As Garcia explained, these men were able to keep the women from going to police by getting them addicted to drugs, and using that addiction as leverage to keep them working for them.

According to these women, men involved as traffickers are smart; they know which women to target, what to tell them, and how to convince them that they need him. In their own words, they felt controlled mentally and physically for a year and a half, until finally going to a domestic violence center.

Funk, in her own words, has recalled, "You feel disgusting, you feel filthy, you feel like you're worth nothing. And then you get physically dependent on a drug that they're offering you... Every day is hell, every day of your life is hell when you're stuck in trafficking.

"It takes a long time to find your self-worth and realize that you can have a life after trafficking, that is it possible."

With their backgrounds, both Funk and Garcia are investing their efforts into the Aspen House, and allowing survivors to connect with others who have made it out of their situations and become confident again.

The Aspen House was named after aspen trees, which are a symbol of sustainability and interconnectedness. Its goal is to alter the trajectory of multigenerational trafficking and help prevent others from having the same experiences.
