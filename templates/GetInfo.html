What is human trafficking?

Human trafficking is defined as: "the unlawful act of transporting or coercing people in order to benefit from their work or service, typically in the form of forced labor or sexual exploitation."

Why is it still a problem?

Human trafficking hurts people. People who are coerced into trafficking are permanantly scarred and exhibit high levels of anxiety, emotional numbness, memory loss, depression, post-traumatic stress disorder, etc.

What can you do to stop human trafficking?

Our invitation is to add the trafficking hotline's phone numberto your contacts: (888)373-7888. By adding this number to your contacts you are better prepared to protect others against the threat of human trafficking.
